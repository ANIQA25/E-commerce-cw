Top Destinations in Singapore

Marina Bay Sands

The opulent Marina Bay Sands resort complex includes a high-end luxury hotel, a mall with a canal running through it and the Marina Bay Sands Skypark Observation Deck. The Skypark's viewing deck and infinity pool are found in the ship that tops the hotel.

Gardens by the Bay

Once you've glimpsed this beautifully designed green space, you won't be able to stay away. Wander through the Bay East Garden, perfect for enjoying the vibrant plant life and escaping the city bustle for a moment.

Singapore Zoo

Billing itself as the world's best rainforest zoo, the Singapore Zoo is a pretty impressive place. The facility is clean and inviting, and the animals appear well treated, with plenty of lush vegetation and habitat space.

Singapore Flyer

The Singapore Flyer, the world's largest giant observation wheel allows you to enjoy a view that encompasses not only the Singapore skyline but as far away as the Spice Islands of Indonesia and Malaysia's Straits of Johor.

Universal Studios Singapore

Universal Studios Singapore occupies 49 acres of Resorts World Sentosa. The park is arranged thematically, with each area paying tribute to a location, film, or television show. Destinations include New York City, Hollywood, Madagascar, and a trip back to Ancient Egypt.

Sentosa Island

For some fun in the sun, Sentosa Island is the place to find it. Siloso Beach is a good spot for getting in beach time, and visitors can play volleyball on free courts or go kayaking and skimboarding. There are several other beach attractions as well, plus an Underwater World aquarium, where you can swim with dolphins.

Clarke Quay

The "center of commerce during the 19th century," Clarke Quay lives up to its legacy as a busy hub. Today, it has a more polished sheen, so after a long day of shopping on Orchard Road, visitors can happily head to Clarke Quay for an evening of waterfront dining and entertainment.

Merlion Park

Singapore's Merlion is the figure of a mythical creature that has the head of a lion and the body and tail of a fish. The Merlion represents the city's humble start as a fishing village combined with its traditional Malay name Singapura, "lion city."

The Maritime Experiential Museum

This indoor-outdoor museum is located right on the water, and it's a great way to explore Singapore's maritime history through fun, interactive exhibits. Before tourists even enter the building, they'll be able to see several ships anchored here.

Chinatown

Singapore's Chinatown neighborhood will bring you right back to China. From the small pop stores to the bright red lanterns, there's excitement in this district. Tourists can visit the Chinese Heritage Centre and see the impressive Sri Mariamman Hindu temple.

Night Safari Singapore

Night Safari Singapore puts a new twist on the traditional zoo experience by introducing visitors to the nocturnal lives of the residents. The park's habitats are divided into four sections, each with its own trail that lets you observe the elusive creatures.

Jewel Changi Airport

Often voted as the best airport in the world, the 10-story-high Jewel Ghangi is not an ordinary transportation hub. In addition to over 300 shops, the airport's most famous feature is the 40-meter-high HSBC Rain Vortex, an indoor waterfall surrounded by over 2,000 trees.

Weather in Singapore

True to its Southeast Asian location, Singapore is characterised by a hot and humid climate. Located just 1 degree north of the equator, it quite naturally enjoys a tropical/equatorial climate. The island does not have clear-cut seasons like summer, spring, autumn and winter. The weather is warm and humid all year round. Rainfall is almost an everyday phenomenon, even during the non-monsoon period.

Singapore in popular culture is known to have two seasons- Wet and Dry. The Dry 'season' usually last from March to August, while the Wet 'season' lasts from September to February. However, there is not fixed weather in Singapore.

Wet Season in Singapore

While there is no distinct wet or dry season in Singapore, monthly variations in rainfall do exists. Higher rainfall occurs from November to January during the wet phase of Northeast Monsoon season Figs.

Rainfall in Singapore shows a marked diurnal variation, with rainfall occurring more frequently during the daytime, particularly in the afternoons when solar heating is strongest.

Specialty Food in Singapore

Singaporean cuisine is as ethnically diverse as its people, blending Malay, Chinese, Indonesian, Indian and western influences.Singapore is especially renowned for its seafood. Chili crab and black pepper crab are two quintessential dishes that dominate the scene. Another favourite is sambal stingray. In the meat category, Hainanese chicken rice is the most popular dish.

Chilli Crab
Hainanese Chicken rice

Chilli Crabs are Crabs cooked in gravy with a tomato chilli base. The steamed crabs are cracked, then stir-fried in a paste comprising of chilli sauce and eggs.

The second dish is Steamed chicken served with rice cooked in chicken stock. The quality of chicken stock is crucial to this dish. The steamed rice is oozing with flavour and a fragrant aroma.

Barbecued stingray has become a popular seafood dish served at hawker stalls. This features stingray meat slated in thick sambal sauce then wrapped in banana leaf to be cooked on a grill.

Festivals in Singapore

Singapore is a country where 'east meets west'. The Lion City's culture is vast, as it includes both Asian and European cultures. Singapore has many areas divided by ethnicity of people like Little India and Chinatown. Singapore's culture is so vast that the country has four official languages. It has attracted many immigrants due to its full respect for human rights and equality. Therefore, there is no doubt that there are many events and festivals in Singapore that are celebrated with zest and zeal.
